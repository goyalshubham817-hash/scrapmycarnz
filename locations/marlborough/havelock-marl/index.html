Frequently Asked Questions — Havelock

How much will I get for my scrap car in Havelock?

The value depends on the make, model, year, condition, and current New Zealand scrap metal prices. We regularly pay between $200 and $15,000. Even completely written-off or non-running vehicles receive competitive offers. Submit your rego number for a free, no-obligation quote specific to your vehicle.

Do you offer same-day removal in Havelock?

Yes — in the vast majority of cases we can collect your vehicle the same day you call or request a quote. Our drivers operate locally in Havelock and the surrounding area seven days a week, including public holidays.

Does the car need a current WOF or registration?

No. Because the vehicle is being scrapped rather than resold for road use, it does not need a current Warrant of Fitness or registration. We buy in any condition — running or not, registered or unregistered, damaged or intact.

Is car removal really free with no hidden charges?

Absolutely. All towing, collection, and transportation costs are covered by us. There are no fees deducted from your payment and no surprise charges on collection day. The price we quote is the price you receive.

How do I notify NZTA when scrapping my car?

You are legally required to notify NZTA by completing the MR15 disposal form. Our team will guide you through this process or handle it on your behalf. This removes the vehicle from your name and protects you from any future fines or liabilities.

Can you buy a car that still has finance owing on it?

Unfortunately we cannot purchase vehicles that are still under a finance agreement. The outstanding loan must be fully settled before we can process the sale. Once finance is cleared, contact us and we will arrange same-day pickup.

What types of vehicles do you accept in Havelock?

We accept all motor vehicles including cars, utes, vans, SUVs, 4WDs, trucks, and campervans of any age or condition. We buy popular New Zealand models such as Toyota Hilux, Corolla, Mitsubishi Outlander, Ford Ranger, Holden Commodore, Subaru Forester, and many more.
