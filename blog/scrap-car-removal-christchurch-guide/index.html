Scrap Car Removal Christchurch: Complete Local Guide

Christchurch is New Zealand's second-largest city and the hub of the South Island. With over 600,000 people in the Canterbury region, it's one of our busiest service areas. Here's your complete guide to scrapping a car in Christchurch — what to expect, how much you'll receive, and how the process works.

Scrap Car Prices in Christchurch (2024)

Christchurch scrap car prices are broadly in line with national averages, though proximity to the Lyttelton port (NZ's busiest freight port) means scrap metal transport costs are lower — which can translate to marginally better offers in some cases. Typical Christchurch prices:

  • Small car (Toyota Vitz, Honda Fit): $300 – $650
  • Medium sedan (Toyota Corolla, Mazda Axela): $500 – $900
  • SUV (Toyota RAV4, Subaru Forester): $800 – $2,200
  • Ute (Toyota Hilux, Mitsubishi Triton): $1,200 – $4,000
  • Van (Toyota Hiace, Ford Transit): $800 – $2,500

Christchurch's Vehicle History: Post-Earthquake Recovery

The 2010–2011 Canterbury earthquakes caused significant vehicle damage across the city. Many vehicles were damaged by falling debris, liquefaction, and subsidence. While most earthquake-related damage was dealt with at the time, the city's recovery and rebuild period drove significant vehicle turnover — creating a large second-hand and scrap vehicle market.

Today, Christchurch has a healthy scrap vehicle market driven by its large population, industrial base, and proximity to major transport infrastructure.
